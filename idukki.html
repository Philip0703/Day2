Idukki

Idukki, nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is a captivating district known for its stunning natural beauty and diverse landscapes. Home to the impressive Idukki Arch Dam, one of the world's highest arch dams, and a series of reservoirs, including Cheruthoni and Kulamavu, the region offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills. The picturesque hill station of Munnar, adorned with expansive tea plantations, and the wildlife-rich Periyar National Park add to Idukki's allure. The district is a haven for nature enthusiasts, with opportunities for boat safaris on Periyar Lake and exploration of spice plantations in Thekkady. Idukki's unique blend of scenic charm, cultural heritage, and vibrant biodiversity makes it a sought-after destination for those seeking a tranquil escape into the heart of Kerala's mountainous terrain.

Top Places to visit

Munnar

Tucked away in the Western Ghats of Kerala, Munnar is a mesmerizing hill station renowned for its enchanting landscapes. The region is blanketed with vast tea plantations that form a patchwork of lush greenery against the rolling hills. Mist-covered mountains, meandering streams, and vibrant flora create a picturesque setting, making Munnar a haven for nature lovers. Beyond the tea estates, visitors can explore the Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, adding a touch of wildlife to the region's natural charm.

Idukki Arch Dam

The Idukki Arch Dam, a marvel of engineering, stands proudly as one of the tallest arch dams in the world. Situated amidst the scenic beauty of Idukki, it spans the Periyar River and creates the vast Idukki Reservoir. The dam not only serves practical purposes in water resource management but also offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding Western Ghats. Its impressive structure and the scenic landscape make it a must-visit destination for both engineering enthusiasts and nature seekers.

Periyar National Park

Nestled around the Periyar Lake, Periyar National Park is a wildlife sanctuary that invites visitors into the heart of Kerala's biodiversity. The sanctuary is renowned for its boat safaris, allowing guests to traverse the serene waters while encountering a variety of wildlife. From elephants and tigers to a myriad of bird species, Periyar National Park offers a unique opportunity to witness the rich fauna of the Western Ghats in their natural habitat.

Thekkady

Adjacent to the Periyar National Park, Thekkady is a captivating destination that combines spice plantations, serene lakes, and vibrant local culture. The aroma of spices fills the air as visitors explore the plantations, and boat rides on the tranquil waters of Periyar Lake provide an opportunity to witness the surrounding wildlife. Thekkady's blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences, including traditional dance performances, offers a holistic and immersive journey for travelers.
