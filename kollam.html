Kollam

Kollam, located in the southern part of Kerala along the Malabar Coast, is a city known for its historical significance, cultural richness, and scenic beauty. The city is situated on the shores of the Ashtamudi Lake, making it a gateway to the picturesque backwaters of Kerala. Kollam has been a prominent trading port since ancient times, fostering cultural exchanges and giving rise to a unique heritage. The city is characterized by colonial-era architecture, vibrant temples, and mosques, reflecting its diverse cultural influences. Ashtamudi Lake, with its labyrinthine network of waterways, is a major attraction, offering enchanting boat cruises through the serene backwaters. Kollam is also known for its traditional cashew processing industry, and the city hosts the annual Kollam Pooram festival, a grand cultural event featuring elephants, traditional music, and fireworks. With its blend of historical charm, natural beauty, and cultural vibrancy, Kollam stands as a compelling destination for those seeking an authentic Kerala experience.

Top Places to visit

Thangassery Beach

Thangassery Beach, nestled along the coastline of Kollam, Kerala, is a serene haven that seamlessly blends natural beauty with historical charm. With its golden sands and clear waters, the beach provides a tranquil setting for relaxation and leisurely strolls. What sets it apart is the rich tapestry of history woven into its landscape, evident in the remnants of Portuguese and Dutch forts and the iconic Thangassery Lighthouse that stands as a testament to centuries past. This historical backdrop adds a unique dimension to the beach experience, inviting visitors to explore the cultural heritage of the region while enjoying the coastal beauty.

Palaruvi Waterfalls

Located near Kollam, Palaruvi Waterfalls is a captivating natural spectacle surrounded by lush greenery. The name "Palaruvi," meaning "stream of milk," aptly captures the milky white appearance of the waterfall as it descends from a considerable height. The journey to Palaruvi involves a trek through the verdant landscape, adding an adventurous element to the experience. The cascading waters and the pristine surroundings make Palaruvi a refreshing haven, enticing nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

Jatayu Earth's's Habitat

Located in proximity to Kollam, Jatayu Earth's's Habitat is a unique rock-themed park featuring the colossal Jatayu sculpture, considered the world's largest bird sculpture. This park seamlessly integrates adventure and mythology, offering activities like rock climbing, trekking, and zip-lining amid stunning landscapes. The park's concept, inspired by the mythical bird Jatayu from the Indian epic Ramayana, adds a cultural and symbolic layer to the adventurous experience, making it a must-visit for both thrill-seekers and those intrigued by mythological narratives.

Thenmala Eco-Tourism

Thenmala, India's first planned eco-tourism destination, is situated near Kollam and offers a harmonious blend of nature, adventure, and cultural experiences. The landscape is adorned with dense forests, meandering rivers, and diverse wildlife, providing a picturesque backdrop for eco-friendly activities. Visitors can enjoy elevated walkways through the canopy, boat rides on the Thenmala Dam, and cultural insights into sustainable living practices. Thenmala Eco-Tourism is a haven for those seeking a holistic and responsible travel experience amidst the beauty of the Western Ghats.
